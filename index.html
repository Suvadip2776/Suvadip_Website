Suvadip Sana

 

I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Statistics and Data Science at Cornell University. I work in the areas of AI alignment and Computational social choice, with a focus on building mathematically grounded frameworks for understanding and improving large-scale decision-making systems.

I am grateful to be advised by Professor Marty Wells, I also work closely with Moon Duchin, and Professor Lionel Levine.

My research aims to quantify fairness, consistency, and value alignment in both voting systems and large language models. I am particularly interested in translating classical ideas from social choice theory (e.g., Arrow’s axioms, Sortition) into practical, measurable tools for modern AI systems.

Featured Projects

  • Quantitative Relaxations of Arrow’s Axioms

    • Introduces real-valued fairness metrics such as σIIA and σUF to measure how closely voting rules satisfy classical social choice axioms.

    • Empirical evaluation on 1000+ real-world elections and synthetic preference profiles (Borda, Plurality, STV, etc.).

  • EigenBench: Measuring Value Alignment in LLMs

    • A benchmark for evaluating alignment using pairwise comparisons and aggregation methods inspired by Bradley-Terry and EigenTrust.

    • Focuses on extracting latent value structures from model behavior.

  • Democratic Preference Optimization (DemPO)

    • A framework for incorporating democratic principles into RLHF-style training pipelines using algorithmic sortition.

  • Second-Order Gradient Alignment for Multi-Task Learning

    • A curvature-aware method for aligning gradients across tasks to achieve Pareto-efficient solutions.
