Suvadip Sana

 

I am a final year Ph.D Candidate in the Department of Statistics and Data Science at Cornell University. I am Grateful to be advised by Marty Wells. I also work closely with Lionel Levine and Moon Duchin. My research falls at the intersection of AI alignment and Computational social choice, with a focus on building mathematically grounded frameworks for understanding and improving large-scale decision-making systems.

Before Moving to Cornell, I completed Masters of Mathematics (M.Math) in 2021 and Bachelor of Mathematics in 2019 from Indian Statistical Institute. My research focuses on quantifying fairness, consistency, and value alignment in both voting systems and large language models. I am particularly interested in pluralistic AI, democratic value alignment, and adapting classical ideas from social choice theory—such as Arrow’s axioms and sortition—to modern AI systems.
